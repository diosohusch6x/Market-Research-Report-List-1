<p><p>The fire-resistant flame-retardant cable market is divided into two main types: halogen-containing cables and halogen-free cables. Halogen-containing cables emit toxic gases when burned, but offer robust fire resistance and cost-effectiveness. In contrast, halogen-free cables are designed to minimize toxic emissions and are preferred in environments where safety and environmental concerns are paramount, despite being typically higher in cost. Both types are crucial for various applications, including construction, automotive, and industrial settings, to ensure fire safety and compliance with regulations.</p></p>
